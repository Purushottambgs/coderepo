About Heart Disease

Learn what heart disease is, its symptoms, common risk factors, and how it can be prevented.

What is Heart Disease?

Heart disease is a general term used for conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels. It includes problems such as coronary artery disease, high blood pressure–related heart damage, heart failure, and other cardiovascular conditions.

Common Symptoms

  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue or weakness
  • Irregular heartbeat
  • Dizziness or fainting
  • Pain in arms, neck, jaw, or back

Major Risk Factors

Lifestyle Factors
  • Smoking
  • Alcohol consumption
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Unhealthy diet
  • Obesity
Medical Factors
  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterol
  • High blood glucose / diabetes
  • Increasing age
  • Family history

How to Prevent It

  • Exercise regularly and stay physically active
  • Maintain healthy body weight
  • Eat a balanced diet low in salt and unhealthy fats
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol intake
  • Monitor blood pressure, cholesterol, and glucose regularly
  • Get timely medical checkups

When to Consult a Doctor

Immediate medical attention is recommended if a person experiences severe chest pain, breathing difficulty, fainting, or very high blood pressure. This system is only for educational and predictive assistance and should not replace professional diagnosis.
